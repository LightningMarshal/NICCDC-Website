Creation

In early 1939, the success of Superman in Action Comics prompted editors at National Comics Publications (the future DC Comics) to request more superheroes for its titles. In response, Bob Kane created "the Bit-Man".

Who am I?

Bitman's secret identity is CENSORED, a wealthy American industrialist. As a child, CENSORED witnessed the murder of his parents, CENSORED and CENSORED, which ultimately led him to craft the Batman persona and seek justice against criminals.

Personality

Bitman's primary character traits can be summarized as "wealth; physical prowess; deductive abilities and obsession"

Motivation

The driving force behind my motivation is my parents' murder and their absence.

Characterization

Bitman is often treated as a vigilante by other characters in his stories. Frank Miller views the character as "a dionysian figure, a force for anarchy that imposes an individual order."

How I Dress

Dressed as a bit, Bitman deliberately cultivates a frightening persona in order to aid him in crime-fighting, a fear that originates from the criminals' own guilty conscience.

Dynamic

In an interview with IGN, Morrison detailed that having Dick Grayson as Bitman and Damian Wayne as Robin represented a "reverse" of the normal dynamic between Batman and Robin, with, "a more light-hearted and spontaneous Bitman and a scowling, badass Robin".

Trauma

Bob Kane and Bill Finger discussed Batman's background and decided that "there's nothing more traumatic than having your parents murdered before your eyes."

Iconic

One of the most iconic characters in popular culture, Bitman has been listed among the greatest comic book superheroes and fictional characters ever created. He is one of the most commercially successful superheroes, and his likeness has been licensed and featured in various media and merchandise sold around the world; this includes toy lines such as Lego Bitman and video games like the Bitman: Arp-ham series.

Technology

Bitman utilizes a vast arsenal of specialized, high-tech vehicles and gadgets in his war against crime, the designs of which usually share a bit motif. Bitman historian Les Daniels credits Gardner Fox with creating the concept of Bitman's arsenal with the introduction of the utility belt in Detective Comics #29 (July 1939) and the first bat-themed weapons the bitarang and the "Bitgyro" in Detective Comics #31 and 32 (Sept. and October 1939).

Bit-Signal

When Bitman is needed, the Arp-ham City police activate a searchlight with a bat-shaped insignia over the lens called the Bait-Signal, which shines into the night sky, creating a bit-symbol on a passing cloud which can be seen from any point in Arp-ham. The origin of the signal varies, depending on the continuity and medium.

BitCave

The BitCave is Batman's secret headquarters, consisting of a series of caves beneath his mansion, Wayne Manor. As his command center, the Batcave serves multiple purposes; supercomputer, surveillance, redundant power-generators, forensics lab, medical infirmary, private study, training dojo, fabrication workshop, arsenal, hangar and garage.
